## Lead / Summary (English translation)
Zhongwai Wenxue Jiaoliu Shi: China–Japan Volume, written by Wang Xiaoping (Professor, School of Chinese Language and Literature, Tianjin Normal University), was published in 2016 by Shandong Education Press as part of the multivolume series "History of Literary Exchanges between China and Other Countries." The book systematically traces the history of literary exchange between China and Japan from classical through modern periods, paying attention to both mainstream and less-studied forms (such as educational primers and miscellaneous texts) that played roles in cross-cultural transmission, and emphasizing the profound influence of exchange on the literary development of both countries.

## Concise summary (English)
Professor Wang Xiaoping’s Zhongwai Wenxue Jiaoliu Shi: China–Japan Volume is a comprehensive scholarly study that traces literary exchanges between China and Japan from ancient times to the modern era. The book treats mainstream genres—poetry, prose, and poetics—while also foregrounding less-studied forms (e.g., primers and miscellanies) that contributed to transmission and reception. Through thematic and chronological chapters, it examines how media (hand-copying, printing), reading and performance practices, and institutional contacts shaped mutual influence and transformation in literary traditions. The volume has been translated into Japanese, is widely cited in comparative-literature and East Asian studies, and is considered an important reference for research on Sino–Japanese cultural relations.
