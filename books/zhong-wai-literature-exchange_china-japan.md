## Concise summary
Wang Xiaoping's "Zhongwai Wenxue Jiaoliu Shi: China—Japan Volume" is a comprehensive scholarly study tracing literary exchange between China and Japan from antiquity through the modern period. The book addresses mainstream genres—poetry, prose, and poetics—while also foregrounding less-studied forms such as educational primers and miscellaneous texts that played a role in cross-cultural transmission. Through thematic chapters and chronological analysis, the volume explores how media (hand-copying, printing), reading and performance practices, and institutional contacts shaped mutual influence and transformation of literary traditions. It has been translated into Japanese and is widely cited in comparative literature and East Asian studies.
